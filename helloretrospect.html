USER SURVEYS

Priorization for the win.

After surveying three experts in the automotive industry, I gain some insights points that allowed me to have a better understanding of what to prioritize for the tool's users. Surveys were centered around 3 topics:

Personal driving experiences

Near collision experiences

New techology experiences

INSIGHTS

Design principles that shaped everything:

Keep it simple (no mental gymnastics required)

Give choices, but not chaos

Prioritize risk clarity

Make it exciting, not exhausting

REFLECTION

If Retrospect had a retrospective.

Expand beyond inner circles

Since the company brought in people that I could survey, my user research participant type was limited. With more time and resources, it would be beneficial to talk to randomized users that fit the target group to get even more honest feedback.

Be more confident of my work

Oftentimes, I would not mention an idea that I had until the end of the meeting, and every time, that was the idea that was actually implemented. As a result, throughout the span of the internship, I felt way more comfortable sharing ideas, even the craziest of ones.

Lean into cross-functional work

I was able to call on my programming skills for the Scenario Creator Tool, giving me extremely valuable experience working alongside the software engineers for the Risk Visualizer Tool.
